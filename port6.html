DIFFERENT TYPES
OF COMPUTERS

INTRODUCTION

Ever since the evolution of humanity’s technology. We’ve eventually made the computer for making much more complex calculations, and as we made more computers we made them much more technologically advanced when it comes to speed, efficiency, and power. Just like the very first supercomputers, it used to have a single CPU and could only have a peak performance of 3 million floating point operations per second (FLOPS). Now on average, a supercomputer can calculate 200 to more than 1000 million floating point numbers per second (Clark, 2011).

DISCUSSION

Based on the table that is filled with data, there is a difference in use between the different types of computers. One example could be their purpose in calculating huge amounts of data. The supercomputer is best suited for the job because it was specifically designed to process and calculate large amounts of data because of the supercomputer’s speed and efficiency to calculate data with the help of parallel computing. Another user could have a need for having a small computer just for their individual wants or needs, in this case, the microcomputer is best suited for it because it is mainly used for surfing the internet, home entertainment, and word processing (Kanwal, 2024).
