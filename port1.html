Computer Science as a Discipline,
Computing Disciplines and Majors

COMPUTER SCIENCE AS A DISCIPLINE

According to Denning et al. (1989), computer science as a discipline conforms to the tradition scientific methods and standards by incorporating the use of theory, experimentation and design. They also said that students can learn to be competent with their coding skills by learning more about the computer’s ins and outs.

5 COMPUTING DISICPLINES
  • Computer Science
  • Information Systems
  • Information Technology
  • Computer Engineering
  • Software Engineering
    • COMPUTER SCIENCE

    Computer Science as a major involves topics such as Data Structures and Algorithms. It dwells in the theoretical aspects of computing. It involves writing programs, designing systems, and creating applications (Proulx et al., 1996).

    INFORMATION SYSTEMS

    Information Systems as a major involves analyzing, designing, implementing and maintaining information systems for businesses so that they can help with the decision making process and the day-to-day operations. Because of this, technology has been been even more integrated with more businesses (Bourgeois, 2014).

    INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY

    According to O’ Brien and Marakas (2006), they say that professionals in information technology are supposed to optimize the use of rare resources in a company or organization using information support to help them with the decision-making.

    COMPUTER ENGINEERING

    Computer Engineering as a major involves applying what they learned theoretically into actual real life applications. So they deal with both software and hardware. They have topics like the binary number system, hard disk and many more (Tokdemir and Cagiltay, 2010).

    SOFTWARE ENGINEERING

    According Sommerville (2011), software engineering is a systematic collection of good practices and techniques to create applications. This involves learning efficient and cost-effective coding skills for software development.

    REACTION

    Before I started learning about the computing disciplines and majors, and computer science as a discipline, I barely had any idea on what the difference was between all the majors such as Computer Science, Information Technology, Information Systems, Computer Engineering and Software Engineering. I also know how important computer science is as a discipline because it helps me understand the computer better, but not just on the surface level but on a deeper level. Learning about this certain discipline helped me realize that computer science is focused more on the theoretical side of computers like binary number systems, bits, and algorithms rather than the applications such as the maintenance for the software or the hardware aspects of it such as making robots or making the next generation chipsets. Although there is still some application like making a website, it is still focused on algorithms, data structures, and generally logic. Knowing this, undergoing computer science will be challenging but at least it will help me learn to be better and competent enough with my people and coding skills that I can endure and be successful in the tech industry. I also realized how there’s a huge difference between the different majors or disciplines. Although all of them involve the use of computers, there’s still a distinct difference between them such as their strengths and weaknesses. Take Computer Science and Computer Engineering for example, the difference between them is that Computer Science focuses more on the theoretical foundations of a computer while Computer Engineering focuses more on low level languages and hardware. As for Information Technology, this discipline is more of like an intersection between the two majors because it involves softwares but they also dwell on maintaining hardware. For Information Systems, it’s almost the same as Information Technology but the main purpose of Information Systems is to help businesses create systems that can help with the decision making process. Lastly, software engineering deals with software and programming. Although it doesn’t dwell too much into the theoretical aspects of a computer, it just deals with engineering software.
