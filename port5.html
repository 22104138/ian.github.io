PORTFOLIO #5

DIFFERENT TYPES
OF MOTHERBOARDS

INTRODUCTION

As the years went on, humanity has gone along and evolved on their technology. And with the rise of technology comes the rise of the different motherboards and their capabilities. To provide a short description to the motherboard, the motherboard is the largest electronic component in a computer that contains and connects most of the electronics (Schmidt, 2019). Back in 1981, the International Business Machines (IBM) released the original motherboard, along with the original Personal Computer (PC). It only had five expansion slots that could only handle 8 bits of information at the time, as opposed to the modern ATX motherboard that has 7 expansion slots (Jones et al., 2003). Knowing this, technology has long evolved during the years with motherboards being far faster and more advanced than when it was first made.

DISCUSSION

With the vast and diverse knowledge about the different types of motherboards and their purpose, we can say that each motherboard has their own purpose. Whether when the user wants to make a computer that’s smaller in size and that’s cost effective then they can use a micro-ATX motherboard . If the user wants to build a standard computer system then they could opt for an ATX motherboard (Mueller, 2003).
