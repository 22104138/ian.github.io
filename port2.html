DATA, INFORMATION, AND INFORMATION SYSTEMS

DATA

Data are basic numerical items that were results from observations, however these items don’t have any context (Zins, 2007). Data consists of patterns that have no meaning, they are the ingredients for the interpretation process (Aamodt & Nygård, 1995).

INFORMATION

Information is the refined data that is has become useful for analysis (Liew, 2007). It is a particular fact or context that is told to someone (Buckland, 1991).

DATA AND INFORMATION

Data is raw, it only exists and has no other significance beyond its existence. It can exist in any shape or form but it does not contain any meaning. Meanwhile information is the data that has been given meaning by ways in relational context. This meaning can be useful but in other cases it could also not be useful (Bellinger et al., 2004).

INFORMATION SYSTEMS

These information systems collect, store, analyze, and distribute data for a specific purpose. This purpose could be for business related reasons (Rainer et al., 2020). Its purpose is to give information to a person or a group need the information (Boaden & Lockett, 1991). Information systems are a crucial investment for any businesses in this age (Pearlson et al., 2024). These systems can create information based on the given date (Ultrich, 2001). These are network systems that provide services to the end-user (Westmark, 2004).

4 KINDS OF INFORMATION SYSTEMS
  • Transaction Processing Systems
  • Management Information Systems
  • Decision Support Systems
  • Executive Information Systems
TRANSACTION PROCESSING SYSTEM

The Transaction Processing System (TPS) is a system used in business to input, enter, store, and process the important details of a business event. After processing, the system will generate information to help assess the business. Because of this, the Transaction Processing System is best used for day-to-day operating routines (Rahmatian, 2002).

MANAGEMENT INFORMATION SYSTEM

Management Information Systems are used for understanding real world problems in business. The Management Information System is used for businesses to gain a competitive advantage, improve on decision making, and strengthen business processes (O’brien & Marakas, 2006). This system gets its data from the output of the TPS and then generates information after processing the data (Gupta, 2010).

DECISION SUPPORT SYSTEMS

Decision Support Systems play a primary role for computers in the decision making process. This information system is primarily used by managers because this system generates insights about management problem solving to improve the effectiveness of the problem solving (Keen, 1980).

EXECUTIVE INFORMATION SYSTEM

The Executive Information System is a system used by executives to have easy access to both internal and external data that is imperative to their success. This information system is also very easy to use and navigate. Although even with its user-friendly interface, it can still have its important functions such as extracting, filtering and compressing important data (Watson et al., 1991).
